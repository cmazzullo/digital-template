Love is Blind + Butter

  • High Concept: One player seeks the piece of toast while blindfolded, the other players try to help them find it.
  • Player Experience Goals: Players will have fun and grow closer by pursuing a common goal while having unequal access to information.
  • Genre: A platformer with analog and digital components.
  • Game play: This game is a platformer, so the player needs to jump onto platforms. If they miss, they will fall to their death. The cursor keys control left and right motion, the spacebar makes the player jump. The goal of the game is to touch the piece of toast. The player with access to the keyboard must wear a blindfold during play.
  • Prototype goal: My goal with this prototype is to explore cooperation between players who have unequal amounts of information. I also wanted to make a game that a 40 person class could participate in.
  • Features: This game can be played by two to about one hundred players. It features authentic blindness mechanics.
  • Setting/Premise: You are a stick of butter, and you love a piece of toast. But, you have no eyes. You'll have to rely on your friends to lead you to your beloved toast.
  • Story: Maybe a love triangle with a jar of jam in the sequel?
  • Target Audience: My target audience is casual players who are looking to socialize with a group game. I am also specifically gearing this towards presentation in front of a class.
  • Play time: About three minutes.
  • Strategies: Players who aren't wearing the blindfold will need to coordinate the information that they're giving so that the blindfolded player won't be overwhelmed. The blindfolded player will need to choose which voices they want to base their decisions on, probably using prior information about the other players.
  • Prototype Assets: All of the visual assets are handmade. The sound assets were taken from the Phaser examples. The platforming code was based on a Phaser example.
  • Prototype Closest Other Game: The most similar game is the platforming Phaser example on Photonstorm's website.
